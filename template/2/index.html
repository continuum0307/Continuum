Case Study: An Overview of Tiktok by SWOT & STP Analysis

Strength

  • Having a clear target market; focuses mainly on young adults who express themselves more on social media, and have more time to spend on video sharing.
  • The social nature of TikTok can draw users that have similar interests together.
  • Equipped with a powerful AI-backed recommendation engine and customer database which enable the company to recommend videos to different users based on their profiles and preferences.
  • Featuring 15-second videos, TikTok can catch users’ attention during their fragmented time.
  • Allows brands to set up corporate accounts to launch media campaigns, share videos, and engage directly with followers/viewers; provides an effective channel for them to build brand affinity and customer engagement.

    • Weakness

  • Unclear profit model; TikTok has yet to provide services that can monetize the traffic.
  • Difficult to filter videos with inappropriate content.
  • Some brands may find it difficult to generate more video views and reach their target consumers when TikTok does not push their videos on the front page; viewers may not have the incentives to follow corporate accounts as brands could not give out discounts and coupons via the TikTok app.

    • Opportunities

  • Huge potential of the short video market in Europe, driven by high penetration of mobile Internet users (76% of population in 2019) and the fact that young generation craves for funny and interesting video clips.
  • Total number of downloads of the TikTok increased a lot in 2019 and this app received high scores in app stores in European countries. Particularly in the past 90 days, the number of downloads of TikTok has ranked top 3 among all free social apps in both google play and app store in Great Britain, Germany, France, Italy and Spain.

    • Threats

  • Many Internet players including Facebook, YouTube and Snapchat have viewed TikTok as their competitor and started to tap the short video market recently by launching their own apps, e.g. Lasso, Likee.
  • May be confronted with setbacks from the local regulatory authorities as it has suffered in Indonesia, India and the US before.
  • TikTok’s business model can be easily copied and adopted by others.
  • May lose audience if other platforms can offer better functions and more exciting content and elements.




    • Segmentation

    We mainly segment the Europe online video platform market:

  • By age group and by device
  • By age group and by behavior

    • Targeting

    According to data above, internet users aged 16 to 24 years spent an average of 168 minutes on mobile internet services every day, which is far more than that of other age groups. Also, smartphones users aged between 16 and 24 years are more likely to use their devices on a daily basis to watch full length videos than other age groups. That’s to say, individuals aged between 16 and 24 years have been accustomed to get access to internet via mobile devices as well as watching videos online.

    TikTok is a mobile application that encourages people to make and watch videos. Therefore, in order to better capture potential users, it would be a good choice if TikTok chooses to target the Europeans aged between 16 and 24. Or we say, Generation-Z.

    Positioning

    In Europe, TikTok is one of the two popular social networks that focus on video making and sharing. The other one is YouTube. There also exist other social networks that enable users to make videos, such as Instagram and Snapchat. However, TikTok still gets a chance to differentiate its positioning from others:

  • PORTABLE.

    • TikTok is a social network designed and conceived exclusively for mobile devices. Although users can access the app from laptops to visualize its contents, the actual video production is accessible only from Smartphones (as the vertical framing of the videos suggests). TikTok users shoot, edit and share the creations via mobile phone, the mobility of which highlights different production styles and dynamics. This brings the experience to the forefront where each user can be at the same time the producer and protagonist of the video.

  • CREATIVE.

    • The videos might be only 15 seconds long, but creativity could be unlimited: there could be a lot of work and planning involved in the production of these videos. TikTok has a series of AR effects that can be used in videos that makes them fun; the Beauty button that correct any aesthetic imperfection; and a variety of filters, etc. In addition to the editing tools, users have a playlist of all the most popular songs to select as BGM. Picking the right song can be a key reason for a video to go viral. As the mission of TikTok says, to capture and present the world creativity, knowledge, and moments that matter in everyday life.

  • DECENTRALIZED.

    • TikTok is a decentralized network of performers with distinctive user portraits. In fact, TikTok is designed to facilitate the collaboration between unacquainted users through interactions. For example, TikTok users can engage with each other through “challenges”. That is, they all make videos attempting to do the same thing such as performing the Git up Dance. The community-created trend is an easy way for people to feel like they belong. TikTok videos represent narratives of collective participation where claiming authorship for one’s work is not nearly as important as participating in the collective experience.
